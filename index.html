About Me, Page 1

Hi! I'm a soft girl with a gentle heart, tahimik, mahiyain, pero sobra akong clingy once I’m comfortable. I’m the type who loves hugs, kisses, and any kind of warm physical touch. Minsan jolly ako, minsan nakakatawa, pero most of the time I’m calm, soft, and quiet.

I’m an overthinker, but I’m also mabait, masayahin, and surprisingly mature kahit playful ako. I have a very long patience, and I always try to understand everyone around me. I may be “mahina sa lahat ng bagay,” pero my heart is strong in loving people genuinely.

My Type (Friend + Personal Type)

I’m the type of friend na palaging nandiyan, the listener, the loyal one, the “mother friend,” the clingy one, and the super protective friend. When I care about someone, I really care deeply. I’m soft, gentle, and full of affection. I give warm presence, quiet comfort, and silent loyalty.

What I Need

I need affection, understanding, honesty, reassurance, and someone who sees my softness as a strength. I don’t ask for much — just warm love and gentle presence.

What I Want in Life

A simple and peaceful life. To be loved genuinely. To be appreciated. To grow and heal slowly.

Adulthood

a softer reset, a braver version of me

The Other Side of Me

Turning 18 made me realize there’s another side of me I’m still learning. I’m not just the jolly, clingy, or moody version people already know — I’m someone who wants to grow, unlearn, and become better in ways that actually matter. I’m starting to understand myself more deeply, even the parts I used to ignore.

Learning Something New

This chapter feels like a season of learning. Learning how to choose myself, how to be patient with my emotions, and how to face unfamiliar things without fear. I want to try new experiences, explore new skills, and allow myself to be a beginner without feeling ashamed.

Walking With God

As I step into adulthood, I also want to grow spiritually. I’m learning to follow God’s word, not perfectly, but sincerely. I want to trust His timing, seek His guidance, and let my faith shape the way I love, choose, and live. This is me choosing growth — with grace, faith, and intention.

What I Hope For

I hope to experience more of life — the quiet lessons, the unexpected joys, and even the hard moments that teach me strength. I want to keep learning, keep believing, and keep becoming someone I’ll be proud of, one step at a time.

My Weaknesses, and My Strength

My Weaknesses

I used to ignore some things that are noticeable in peoples eyes, Knowing that i hate it when it makes me cry, or if its makes me feel alone and it’s also hard when there is no one who's willing to listen to you when everything feels so heavy, and when you just want to lean on someone you feel safe with—just to let out what you want to say what you’re feeling. It’s also hard when no one tells you, “You can do this. Of course you can. I’m proud of you. But knowing that you're weak. you can't speak, you can't say anything that you wanted to say just because you know that they are not going to understand you on what your going to say just to understood. like you also wanted to say something that burdens you, or just to vent out on someone, seeking for someones heartfelt words and support just to feel safe.

My Strengths

My strength comes from having someone who understands the situation I struggle with, or who sees me at my lowest and turns that into my strength. I admit that one of my weaknesses is how much words affect me—when they understand me in a situation, whether it’s a small or big part of my life, knowing that I am heard and understood. It’s hard to pretend that you can still handle everything and that you won’t give up, even when it feels like you already are. But my true strength is being seen in the little things—in words that cheer me up and genuinely give me more courage and strength within myself.

My Weakness and My Strength When comes to friendships and etc.

My weakness with the people I interact with is how they judge or look at you from head to toe—as if they have so many things they want to say about you but can’t say them out loud. You know how hard it is to deal with someone who has an ugly attitude; even if they look good, they will still judge you. My strength with people, on the other hand, is those who have understanding and empathy for others. Sometimes, the ones who seem bad are actually the truly kind ones, and the ones who appear good in people’s eyes are the ones who are truly unkind. As they say, don’t judge people and don’t belittle others. Many people suffer while trying to build themselves up just to be seen positively by others, but deep inside, they are carrying something heavy that they can’t express. That’s why we should spread respect and understanding toward others and be open to every person’s opinion.

Be kind always.
